## Book Overview

The book covers:

1. **Foundation** — What LLMs are, how they work, tokenization, training
2. **Evaluation** — How to measure model quality (exact, semantic, AI-as-judge, human)
3. **Prompt Engineering** — Techniques to get better outputs
4. **RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)** — Grounding models in external knowledge
5. **Fine-tuning** — Adapting models to specific tasks
6. **Inference & Deployment** — Serving models efficiently
7. **Agents** — Tool use, planning, multi-agent systems

---

## Key Themes

- **Evaluation is the hardest part** — there is no single metric; use a combination
- **Context is everything** — most capability comes from what you put in the prompt
- **Retrieval > memorization** — RAG is often better than fine-tuning for knowledge tasks
- **Latency and cost matter** — real systems need to balance quality vs speed vs cost
